## The Unstoppable Math Formally Known as Compounding

Albert Einstein allegedly called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world," and for good reason. While simple interest only yields returns on your initial principal, compounding allows you to earn returns on your returns. Over time, the growth curve transitions from a steady slope to an explosive, exponential vertical line. 

The formula for compound interest is:  
$A = P(1 + r)^n$

*   **A** = Final Amount
*   **P** = Principal (Starting Balance)
*   **r** = Interest Rate (per period)
*   **n** = Number of Periods
